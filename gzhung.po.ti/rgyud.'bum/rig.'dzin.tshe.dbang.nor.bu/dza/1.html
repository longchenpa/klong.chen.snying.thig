[ Line 3-3 has the words: bam po thog ma zug pa/ The same words appear in the same place in the versions of this text found in the other NGB editions. However, no ending of this bam po is indicated later on, nor are any subsequent bam po marked. Interestingly, we have a similar example in Volume Pha: Rig 'dzin Tshe dbang nor bu Pha 6 , 221a ff., where we find a reference announcing the start of a first “bam po”, given after the Tibetan title. No further “bam po” appear to be marked, and the Kaneko catalogue appears to indicate that the same is true of the gTing skyes edition. However, the Virginia catalogue notes that the mTshams brag edition of this text (our Pha 6 ) has full references to five “bam po”. It would seem likely that those references were at some stage removed from an ancestor of our and gTing skyes’s editions: in the case of our Dza 1, the same process may have occurred but at an earlier stage so that all the editions had the further bam po references removed. ]
