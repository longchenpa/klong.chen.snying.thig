hu'm..cod

Codicological information on Volume Hu'm.


Modern library pagination and markings

In this volume, the western numbering is written in blue pencil numbers. There is a rectangle of cloth at the front of the volume, of the same type used in Volume A, with what appears to be a Western style design on it. On this material is stuck a rectangular IOL paper sticker, which is white with red borders (as in other volumes, with a heart-shaped decoration in each corner). We find red handwriting, "319 pages"; blue writing, "MISC. COLLN. 30+", and in large Tibetan writing, "rgyud hu'm.".

Paper composition

Some sheets are thicker than others, and some are of more than one layer.

Size of folios

The size of the plain sheets is 573 x 125mm. There is some size variation but so many sheets are damaged (see below), it is difficult to comment on the original dimensions of many of them. It would also appear that some sheets were not cut very straight, but again, comment is difficult given the subsequent damage.

Pagination

Page details are given in words written sideways in black ink, to the left of the writing on each recto side.

Page settings

Most sheets, as in other volumes, have 7 lines of black ink writing, with double red vertical lines to the sides of the writing. The writing is not always exactly square on the page (eg. 12b, where the last few lines go up and down in a wavy line, and 32a, where all the lines go up slightly from the left-hand to the right-hand-side of the sheet).

Ornaments, openings and punctuation

Double yig mgo are found in the upper left corner of all recto sides, apart from 1-3. gNyis and gsum each have a triple yig mgo, and the verso side of gcig also has a triple yig mgo.

There is more use of rin chen spungs shad (U0F11) after the first syllables on a line than in most other volumes (where tsheg shad (U0F0F) or nyis tsheg shad (U0F10) are more common), especially in the first part of the volume.

Handwriting

The style of writing in the volume is generally slightly larger than in many of the other volumes, and we find more cases of 'greng bu written upright to avoid syllables above (although in many instances, the upright 'greng bu seem to be purely a matter of style), or of gi gu displaced for similar reasons.

State of preservation

The sheets in this volume are badly damaged, becoming worse as the volume progresses. Except for the first 20 folios, all the sheets have been wrapped in white tissue paper (several folios together) to protect them. Even some of the early folios have large chunks of edges torn off or creased up. In the early folios, the main damage is to the edges and the writing area is rarely badly affected, although 13-15 have a hole near the top, and parts of the top layers of paper from the adjacent sheets have torn away and stuck on, so that several syllables on both sides of the folios are missing or illegible. From 22, most folios have a piece torn from the bottom left corner and the writing near this part of the sheets is often washed out and smudged. 30b has an attempted repair of a hole with a piece of paper stuck to the top edge. From 48, the entire bottom edges of the sheets are badly torn, and the bottom parts of the sheets become increasingly washed out. Serious tears and washing out start from 105, especially afflicting the bottom (recto) or top (verso) lines of the pages. Some of the damage is due to thin pieces of the top layer of paper having torn off and been stuck to the adjacent sheet, presumably due to water damage, thus entirely removing the syllables of one page and obscuring the covered syllables of the adjacent page. Furthermore, not only are syllables often washed out, but frequently, the ink from the syllables on adjacent folios has blotted onto them and the reversed syllables from these adjacent folios are superimposed upon the sheet, making the washed out syllables of the original page even more difficult to read. 124 and 132 have large pieces torn off, missing much of the recto bottom and verso top lines. From the 140s, the right hand edges of sheets are also badly torn, but often this does not affect the writing area badly. 148 has a large piece torn from the bottom, affecting the writing of the recto side’s bottom and the verso top lines, and 148b also has a large section of the top right of the page missing - the top layer of much of the sheet has been torn off and stuck to 149a. 152-165 all have the bottom left corners missing and much of the bottom of the sheets damaged. Considerable damage to the bottom sections of the sheets continues through the rest of the volume. The writing is mainly legible in the earlier sheets but the damage is generally worse as the volume progresses. Virtually all the sheets have some syllables partly washed out. The upper parts of verso sides 181b, 184b, 186-188b, 191b, 195-7b, 200b, 204-6b, 211b, 218b, 222b are missing syllables, as are the bottom parts of recto sides 185a, 187-189a, 193a, 196-8a, 202a, 204-6a, 212a, 218-9a, 223a. 182 has the bottom of the folio torn off, so the writing is missing on both sides of the folio, while 183a has a layer of paper from 182 stuck to it, obscuring much of the bottom of the page. 192 has the bottom left corner torn off, with part of two lines of writing on both sides missing. 201 has the bottom part of the page torn off, and 201b is also missing part of the top layer of the remaining sheet. 203 similarly has part of the bottom of the page torn off, and both sides have part of the top layers of paper missing. 214b has the top layer of the right-hand-side of the edge of the page missing, so that a few syllables per line are absent. 215 and 216 have had the whole right-hand-side of the folio torn off, and are missing several syllables per line on each side. 216b is also missing text through the removal of strips of the top layer of paper, through washing out and through the sticking on of another piece of paper from another sheet. Altogether, about a fifth of the writing is affected. 217 has lost strips of the top layers of paper on both sides and some of the writing is washed out and smudged. 220b has a large strip of the top layer of paper torn off and attached to 221a, affecting about a quarter of the top four lines of 220b and a similar area of the bottom four lines of 221a. A similar layer of paper is missing from 224a, transferred to 223b, while a small strip is torn from 223b and stuck to 224a. The same problem of paper transferred from page to page affects the following sides: 224b and 225a, 225b and 226a. Tearing at the bottom of the folios affects the writing on 226-235, with 227b also missing syllables through washing out and removal of a strip of the top layer of paper, and 228-230 each have a significant part of the bottom right corner torn off. About a third of 231 is torn off, from the bottom. All the folios from here up to 273 are also missing text due to the bottom half of each sheet being affected by tearing, washing out or the transference of strips of paper from one side to the next. 242, for instance, is missing much of two lines of text at the bottom of the recto side and two at the top of the verso side, and many other folios are as badly or worse affected. 260a is missing much of the bottom three lines of writing. All the folios are badly torn at the bottom, with large chunks of edges ripped off, but despite this and the washing out, much of the writing remains mostly legible. Some sheets also have red ink blotches, although these do not obscure the black writing. Many of the folios from the 250s have white spotting in the water damaged areas, which in places makes the writing even less clear. There is a section torn from the top of 282, so that a few syllables are missing. 283-4 are less damaged than most sheets, the tears not extending into the writing area. 288 is torn into two pieces, down the middle of the page. 302 has much of the bottom part of the folio torn off, and all the following folios up to the end of the volume have part of the bottom of the sheet torn away, especially affecting the left hand bottom corner, and in most cases part of at least one line of writing on each side. Problems with blotching, washing out and paper sticking to adjacent sheets continues to obscure other sections of the writing on these folios.

Microfilm

Fragments of torn pages conclude the volume on the microfilm, but the original does not have loose fragments at the end.

Cover folios

There is no blue lacquered front folio in this volume. At the front of the volume, there is a rectangle of loose cloth, on which is stuck a rectangular IOL paper sticker (see above) with the volume identification.

Index Leaves

General Information

There are no index leaves in this volume.

Decorated first folios: construction

The first folio, gcig, is a laminate of about 13 sheets, 570 x 125 mm. It is badly frayed and torn around the edges.

The recto side is plain azure blue lacquer, but with gold margins ruled and the collection, volume, and folio identifications given at the left border, in gold writing. The box made by the ruled lines measures 415 x 67 mm, with the vertical lines not at exact right angles to the horizontal lines, but coming down slightly to the left. The blue sheet is torn at the edges, the blue lacquer is rubbed off in patches in the centre of the page, and there is one hole which goes down through several sheets. Thick crossed stitches attach the folio together, two along the top, five along the bottom, and one to the left and the right of the folio.

Decorated first folios: frames [ TITLE PAGE]

The verso side of gcig (1b) is blue lacquer, with elaborate intertwining straight gold lines around the outside of the page, outlining exactly the same interlocking key design as we find in other volumes. The upper sheet has a rectangular window cut out for the text, 445 x 74mm, outlined (around the frame) with a red paint line. One section (about 130 mm) of the upper window frame is torn off.

Decorated first folios: window curtain

There is a silk cover fitted over the window, with three layers sewn together. Horizontal yellow stitching sewing the window covers onto the sheets beneath is visible in the gap where the upper frame is torn. The yellow layer is at the top, with gold thread around the outside, and it measures 422 x 65mm visible beneath the blue frame (the upper part tucks under the frame above, and a further 22mm is visible in the gap where the upper frame is torn). A blue layer is the middle layer, with white thread around the outside, measuring 432 x 70mm visible. The third and final layer beneath is orange, with green thread around the outside, measuring 438 x 76mm visible. They are not exact rectangles of material, the orange goes in very slightly at the left-hand side near the bottom, and bulges out on the right-hand side. It does not quite cover the two sides of the window.

Decorated first folios: window layout

Beneath the cover is a double lined gold rectangle drawn on black lacquer, with 3 lines of gold writing, split across two sides of the page. There are miniatures to the left and right, besides which the areas for writing are bounded by vertical strips with a regular pattern of gold lines, in this case, hexagon and dot design (see "General Information on Volumes") with 8 dots surrounding each central dot. The larger miniature in the centre, which breaks up the page, is bounded to the left and right by a thin double vertical gold line, outlining a rectangle, 82 x 70mm. The lefthand miniature is within a rectangle 51 x 70mm, the righthand miniature's rectangle is 50 x 70mm. The legends for the miniatures are written below each, within the border of gold lines outside the window. Each legend is within double ornamenal brackets:

[first, to the left:] {{ [yig mgo x 1]/rig 'dzin tshe dbang nor bu la na mo//}} [second, to the left of centre:] {{/lha lcam man [next sylls rubbed out; presumably: dha ra ba/] [yig mgo x 1]/sangs rgyas gnyis pa oyan [then continues to the right of centre, on the other side of the gold vertical strip transversing the text:] rje/ mkha'gro ye shes mtsho rgyal//}} [third, to the right:] {{ [yig mgo x 1]/[first syll rubbed out and unclear] chan [or chen; there is no clearly written 'greng bu, but the gold line above might be obscuring it] rdo rje legs pa}} [usual title for rdo rje legs pa is: dam can rdo rje legs pa. It is unlikely (but not impossible) that chan would be written for can. Could it have been given as, dam chen...? Nebesky-Wojkowitz (318) mentions that he also has the title, "dgra lha chen po", so dgra lha chen is a possibility here. Another title which would probably not fit here is: "dge bsnyen rdo rje legs pa" (Nebesky-Wojkowitz 154) - could we have dge bsnyen chen..."? I do not think we have enough space for all these syllables, and even dgra lha would have difficulty fitting - dam would seem more likely, but it does not look like this. Is either dpal chen or thob chen a likely possibility for rdoe legs?]

Codicological notes on Hu'm..1

at beginning of text



Size of folios

The second folio, gnyis, measures 570 x 122mm.

Decorated second folios

The recto side of gnyis is blue lacquer, with 4 lines of gold writing, within a double gold rectangular box, the outer measuring 476 x 76 mm. The margins are ruled as usual, with the collection, volume and folio identification to the left. The box is not an exact rectangle; the vertical lines are not at exact right angles to the horizontal lines, coming down slightly to the left. The folio is a laminate of about 7 layers, with thick cross stitches visible on both sides, two to the top and bottom, one to the left and right. The folio is badly torn and frayed at the bottom, and the bottom right corner. The verso side is ordinary paper.

Page settings

As mentioned above, 2a has 4 lines of gold writing. 2b is ordinary paper. 2b - 6a have double red lined rectangular boxes and margins; in the case of 2b and 3a, with an inner double red lined rectangular box. 2b and 3a each have 4 lines of black writing; 3b - 5b, have 5 lines of slightly smaller writing, and 6a, 6 lines of writing. The boxes are not very exact rectangles. Sometimes, the lines overshoot the boxes. The boxes measure as follows. 2b: 498 x 80 mm; 3a: 493 x 80mm; 3b: 490 x 78 mm; 4a: 480 x 78mm right/82mm left; 4b: 485 top/490 bottom x 78mm right/80mm left; 5a: 490 x 78mm right/80mm left; 5b: 490 top/494 bottom x 79mm right/82mm left; 6a: 484 x 76. 6b starts the usual style of red margins down each side and 7 lines per side.
