Translation

Here begins the Guru Yoga of the Heart Essence (snying tig gi bla ma'i rnal 'byor pa zhugs so).
Homage to the Guru, Yidam, and Ḍākinī (bla ma yi dam mkha' 'gro rnams la phyag 'tshal lo).

Visualization:

Oneself is visualized as a yidam deity, placed at the crown of all sentient beings (rang nyid yi dam ltar ba skyon sa pa'i spyi bor). Upon a jeweled throne with sun, moon, and lotus (rin po che'i khri dang nyi zla pad+ma'i steng du), resides the root guru, inseparable from Orgyen Padma (rtsa ba'i bla ma dang o rgyan pad+ma dbyings med kyi ngo bo), in the form of Great Vajradhara, with a sky-colored body, holding a vajra and bell (rdo rje 'chang chen po nam mkha'i ma dog can rdo rje dang du'i la bya' rdzin pa). The body is adorned with silks, bone ornaments, and various precious ornaments (dar dang dus pa dang rin po che'i brgya pa).
The consort is Ḍākinī Yeshe Tsogyal, adorned with bone ornaments and silks (yum mkha' 'gro mtsho rgyal da ma ra mo rus pa' rgyan dang ba cas pa). In her right hand, she holds a white yak-tail fan, performing activities (phyag g.yas ya bal 'khra+tsad cid spyod pa), and in her left hand, a nectar-filled skull cup (bdud rtsis bkang bag yon pas ya bal stob pa).
To their right are Padma Ladröl Tsal and Rangjung Dorje, resembling Jampé Yang (pad+ma las 'brel rtsal dang rang byung rdo rje 'jam pa'i dbyangs dang 'dra ba). The right hand holds a ritual dagger, and the left holds the Nyingthig text upon a lotus (g.yas ral gri dang g.yon ut+pa la'i steng na snying tig gi po ti bsnams pa).
To their left are Gyaltsé Lekdenpa and Shakya Zhönnu, resembling Avalokiteśvara, with white bodies, one face, and four arms (g.yon na rgyal sras legs pa dang shAkya gzhon nu gnyis spyan ras gzigs dang 'dra ba sku mdog dkar po zhal gcig phyag bzhi pa). The upper two hands are joined at the heart (dang po gnyis thugs kar thal mo sbyar ba), the lower right hand holds a crystal rosary (g.yas 'og ma chu), and the lower left hand holds a white lotus (g.yon 'og mas pad+ma dkar po 'dzin pa).
In front are Rolpé Dorje and Dharma Dorje, resembling Vajrapāṇi, with blue bodies, one face, two hands, holding a vajra and bell (mdun du rol ba'i rdo rje dang dar ma rdo rje gnyis phyag rdor dang 'dra ba sku mdog mthing kha zhal gcig phyag gnyis rdo rje dang dril bu 'dzin pa).
Behind are Namkha Dorje and Tsultrim Bumpa, resembling Great Compassion Stirring the Depths, with the right hand holding a nectar-filled vase (rgyab tu nam mkha' rdo rje dang tshul khrims 'bum gnyis thugs rje chen po dong sprug dang 'dra ba g.yas bdud rtsi'i bum pa) and the left hand holding a ruby rosary at the heart (g.yon pad+ma rA ga'i phreng ba thugs kar 'dzin pa).
At the guru’s crown, upon a throne of sun, moon, and lotus (gu ru'i spyi gtsug tu nyi zla pad+ma'i gdan gyi steng du), resides Vidyādhara Śrī Siṃha, with a human-colored body, one face, two hands, in the gesture of Vairocana (rig 'dzin shrI sing ha sku mdog mi sha kha zhal kyi gcig phyag gnyis rnam snang gi phyag rgya can). Wearing maroon silk robes and the attire of a paṇḍita, seated in the vajra posture (dar dur smrig gi na bza' gsol ba paN+Di ta'i cha byad can rdo rje'i skyil krung du bzhugs pa).
At his crown is Vidyādhara Garab Dorje, with a white body, one face, two hands, the right hand pointing to the sky in the gesture of subduing (rig zla 'dzin dga' rab rdo rje sku mdog dkar po zhal gcig phyag gnyis g.yas sdigs mdzub nam mkhar gdengs pa), the left hand in the gesture of granting refuge (g.yon skyabs sbyin gyi phyag rgya can). The hair is bound in a topknot, adorned with the six bone ornaments, seated in the heroic posture (dbu skra thor gtsug dang rus pa'i rgyan drug gsol ba zhabs sems dpa'i skyil krung du bzhugs pa).
At his crown is Great Vajradhara, with a sky-colored body, holding a vajra and bell, adorned with the six bone ornaments (de'i spyi gtsug tu rdo rje 'chang chen po nam mkha'i mdog can rdo rje dang dril bu 'dzin cing rus pa'i rgyan drug gsol ba).
At his crown are the five families, yab-yum, holding attributes corresponding to their families (de'i spyi bor rigs lnga yab yum phyag mtshan rigs dang mthun pa).
At their crown is dharmakāya Samantabhadra and consort, with a blue body, one face, two hands, holding a vajra and bell, in union, adorned with various ornaments, seated in the vajra posture (de'i spyi gtsug tu chos sku kun bzang yab yum sku mdog mthing kha zhal gcig phyag gnyis rdo rje dang dril bu 'dzin pas 'khril sbyor rgyan sna tshogs kyis brgyan pa rdo rje'i skyil krung gis bzhugs pa).

All are surrounded by the lineage gurus connected to the teachings and all buddhas and bodhisattvas, blessing and guiding (de la chos 'brel yod tshad kyi bla ma brgyud pa rnams dang sangs rgyas dang byang chub sems dpa' thams cad kyis byin gyis rlob cing 'dren pa'i tshul du bskor nas bzhugs pa).

At their crowns, visualize OM (de rnams kyi spyi bor oM),
at their throats, A (mgrin par AH),
and at their hearts, HUM (thugs kar hU~M bsam).

From the HUM at the heart, light rays emanate (thugs ka'i hU~M las 'od zer 'phros),
invoking all buddhas and bodhisattvas of the ten directions, the guru, yidam, deity assemblies,
ḍākinīs, and dharma protectors, along with the three supports and their palaces (phyogs
bcu'i sangs rgyas dang byang chub sems dpa' bla ma yi ga dam lha tshogs mkha' 'gro chos
skyong rten gsum pho brang dang bcas pa byon par bsam).

Recite VAJRA SAMA DZA (badz+ra sa mA dza), DZA HUM BAM HO (dzahU~M baM ho),
inviting and dissolving them (brjod pas spyan drang zhing bstim par bya'o).

Offer the seven-branch prayer and the outer, inner, and secret
offerings (de la yan lag bdun pa dang phyi nang gsang gsum gyi mchod pa 'bul).
Then, with devotion and aspiration, offer the following supplications (de
nas mos gus dang ldan pas gsol ba 'debs pa ni):

OM A HUM

In the formless, boundless, primordially pure dharmakāya (dngos med mtha' bral ka dag chos kyi sku),
Self-aware, clear, and spontaneously accomplished sambhogakāya (rang rig gsal dwangs lhun grub longs spyod rdzogs),
All-pervading, liberated, and manifold nirmāṇakāya (kun khyab shar grol sna tshogs sprul pa'i sku),
I bow to the gurus of the three times, the self-aware gurus (dus glu ka'i gsum rang rig bla ma rnams la 'dud).
In the pure palace of the non-conceptual dharmadhātu (dmigs med chos dbyings dag pa'i pho brang du),
I offer supplications to dharmakāya Samantabhadra and consort (chos sku kun bzang yab yum la gsol ba 'debs).
Bless me to realize the primordially pure essence (ngo bo ka nas brgya dag par byin gyis rlobs).
In the palace of spontaneous, self-luminous light (lhun grub rang gsal 'od kyi pho brang du),
I offer supplications to the five-family sambhogakāya yab-yum (longs sku rigs lnga yab yum la gsol ba 'debs).
Bless me to manifest awareness as the body (rig pa sku ru 'char bar byin gyis zhe bzhi rlobs).
In the palace of impartial compassion (thug rje phyogs ris med pa'i pho brang du),
I offer supplications to the nirmāṇakāya Vajradhara (sprul sku rdo rje 'chang la gsol ba 'debs).
Bless me to empty the grasping of appearances and mind (snang sems 'dzin pa stongs par byin gyis rlobs).
In the palace of the blazing fire of yogic practice in the charnel ground (dur khrod rnal 'byor me ri 'bar ba'i pho brang du),
I offer supplications to Vidyādhara Garab Dorje (rig 'dzin dga' rab Do rje la gsol ba 'debs).
Bless me to meet the ultimate reality (chos nyid don dang mjal bar byin gyis rlobs).
In the charnel ground of India’s Sosa Ling (rgya gar so sa gling gi dur khrod du),
I offer supplications to Vidyādhara Śrī Siṃha (rig 'dzin shrI sing ha la gsol ba 'debs).
Bless me to abide in the great bliss without union or separation (bde chen 'du 'bral med par byin gyis rlobs).
In the palace of the glorious Lotus Light (dpal ri pad+ma 'od kyi pho brang du),
I offer supplications to Orgyen Padma and consort (o rgyan pad+ma yab yum la gsol ba 'debs).
Bless me to realize innate wisdom (lhan skyes ye shes rtogs par byin gyis rlobs).
In the palace of the treasure place, the colorful rock (gter gnas khra mo brag gi pho brang du),
I offer supplications to the Khandro Nyingthig itself (mkha' 'gro snying thig nyid la gsol ba 'debs).
Bless me to accomplish the remainderless aggregates (phung po lhag med 'grub par byin gyis rlobs).
In the palace of the perfectly pure two accumulations (tshogs gnyis rnam par dag pa'i pho brang du),
I offer supplications to Padma Ladröl Tsal and Rangjung Dorje (las 'brel rtsal dang rang byung rdo rje la gsol ba 'debs).
Bless me to accomplish the two benefits for self and others (bdag gzhan don gnyis 'grub par byin gyis rlobs).
In the palace of entering generation and completion stages together (bskyed rdzogs zung du 'jug pa'i pho brang du),
I offer supplications to Lekdenpa and Shakya Zhönnu (legs ldan pa dang shAkya gzhon zhu la gsol ba 'debs).
Bless me to manifest impartial benefit for beings ('gro don phyogs med 'byung bar byin gyis rlobs).
In the supreme abode where all reside, the palace of the teachings (gar bzhugs gnas mchog chos kyi pho brang du),
I offer supplications to Gyalwa Yungtön Rolpé Dorje (rgyal ba g.yung ston rol pa'i rdo rje la gsol ba 'debs).
Bless me to attain the stability of generation and completion stages (bskyed rdzogs brtan pa thob par byin gyis rlobs).
In the palace where all appearances arise as the dharmakāya (snang srid chos skur shar ba'i pho brang du),
I offer supplications to Dharma Vajra and Namkha Dorje (dar ma badz+ra nam mkha' rdo rje la gsol ba 'debs).
Bless me to ripen impartial beings (phyogs med gdul byar smin par byin gyis rlobs).
In the palace of clarity and emptiness, free from grasping (gsal stong 'dzin pa bral ba'i pho brang du),
I offer supplications to Tsultrim Bumpa and Dharmalakṣa (tshul khrims 'bum dang d+harma lak+Sha la gsol ba 'debs).
Bless me to increase experiential realization (nyams rtogs gong du 'phel bar byin gyis rlobs).
In the palace of the central heart channel (snying dbus ma ba d+hA ti'i pho brang du),
I offer supplications to the kind root guru (drin can rtsa ba'i bla ma la gsol ba 'debs).
Bless me to spontaneously accomplish the five bodies (sku lnga lhun gyis 'grub par byin gyis rlobs).
In the palace of one’s own perfectly pure body (rang lus rnam par dag pa'i pho brang du),
I offer supplications to the assembly of peaceful and wrathful ḍākinīs (zhi khro DAk+ki'i tshogs la gsol ba 'debs).
Bless me to perfect experiential practice (nyams len tshad du skyol bar byin gyis rlobs).
In the palace of perfectly pure commitments (dma tshig rnam par dag pa'i pho brang du),
I offer supplications to the dharma protectors and treasure guardians (bka' srung gter bdag rnams la gsol ba 'debs).
Bless me to be free from adverse conditions and obstacles (rkyen ngan bar chad med par byin gyis rlobs).
I take refuge in the precious guru-buddha (bla ma sangs rgyas rin po che la skyabs su mchi).
Bless me to release the mind of self-grasping (bdag 'dzin blo yis thongs par byin gyis rlobs).
Bless me to give rise to the uncontrived mind (dgos med rgyud la skye bar byin gyis rlobs).
Bless me to realize the unborn mind in a single moment (rang sems skye med cig char rtogs par byin gyis rlobs).
Bless me to purify delusion in its own place ('khrul pa rang sar dag par byin gyis rlobs).
Bless me to manifest all appearances as the dharmakāya (snang srid chos skur 'char bar byin gyis rlobs).
Recite the hundred names as much as possible (mtshan 'bum ci gang 'don).

OM A HUM MAHĀ GURU SAMANTABHADRA YAB-YUM NAMO HUM

During these supplications, nectar flows from the bodies of the lineage gurus,
descending from one’s crown (zhes gsol ba 'debs pa'i dus su bka' brgyud bla ma
rnams kyi sku las bdud rtsi byung nas rang gi spyi bo nas babs), purifying all
obscurations of the three doors (sgo gsum gyi sdig sgrib thams cad dag). Emerging
from the soles of the feet and toes, the body, inside and out, is filled with the
nectar of wisdom (rkang mthil dang sen mo'i sbug nas mar thon nas lus phyi nang
thams cad ye shes kyi bdud rtsis gang bar bsam mo).

Then, the guru, yidam, ḍākinī, and dharma protectors dissolve into Orgyen
Padma and consort (de nas bla ma yi dam mkha' 'gro chos skyong srung ma
rnams o rgyan pad+ma yab yum la bstim). From the three places of the guru
yab-yum, OM, A, HUM, accompanied by light rays, emerge and dissolve into
one’s own three places (bla ma yab yum gyi gnas gsum nas oM AH hU~M gsum
'od zer dang bcas pa byung nas rang gi gnas gsum du thim), purifying the
obscurations of the three doors and accomplishing the siddhis of body,
speech, and mind (sgo gsum gyi sgrib pa dag sku gsung thugs kyi dngos
grub thob par bsam).

Then, the guru yab-yum descends and dissolves into the root guru at the
eight-petaled lotus at one’s heart (de nas bla ma yab yum mar byon nas
snying pad+ma 'dab brgyad kyi steng du rtsa ba'i bla ma bstim mo).

Textual Context:

This text is another Guru Yoga from the Khandro Nyingthig within the Nyingthig Yabzhi, a terma cycle revealed by Padmasambhava and Yeshe Tsogyal, compiled by Longchenpa. It emphasizes visualizing the practitioner as a yidam at the crown of all sentient beings, a hallmark of Khandro Nyingthig’s universal compassion. The practice integrates tantric iconography (Vajradhara, yab-yum, lineage figures) with Dzogchen’s non-dual aim (rigpa).
The supplicatory prayers invoke key lineage figures (Samantabhadra, Garab Dorje, Śrī Siṃha, Padmasambhava, etc.), aligning with Dzogchen’s Menngagde emphasis on lineage transmission and ultimate realization.

Visualization Categories:

Rang dam yi dam dmigs pa (Self-Visualization as Deity): Explicitly stated as the practitioner visualizing themselves as a yidam at the crown of all sentient beings (rang nyid yi dam ltar ba skyon sa pa'i spyi bor), a unique feature of Khandro Nyingthig emphasizing universal compassion.
Bla ma’i spyi bo’i dmigs pa (Guru Visualization Above Practitioner): The root guru as Vajradhara, with Yeshe Tsogyal, is visualized at the practitioner’s crown, followed by a lineage hierarchy (Śrī Siṃha, Garab Dorje, Vajradhara, Samantabhadra).
Brgyud pa’i zhing khams dmigs pa (Lineage Visualization): A detailed refuge field includes lineage figures (Padma Ladröl Tsal, Rangjung Dorje, etc.), buddhas, bodhisattvas, and the five-family yab-yum, culminating in Samantabhadra.
Sems can gyi gnas yod dmigs pa (Universal Visualization): The practitioner as yidam at the crown of all beings and the dissolution of all into Orgyen Padma reflect universal purification, akin to the White A’s light rays purifying sentient beings.
Bzhin gnas gnas pa’i dmigs pa (Dissolution into Non-Dual Awareness): The guru yab-yum dissolves into the practitioner’s heart, resting in non-dual awareness.

Connection to Khandro Nyingthig and White A:

Khandro Nyingthig: This text’s unique feature of visualizing the practitioner as a yidam at the crown of all sentient beings (skyon sa pa'i spyi bor) directly aligns with the Sems can gyi gnas yod dmigs pa category, emphasizing universal compassion. The dissolution into Orgyen Padma mirrors the Inner Guru Yoga’s transformation of sentient beings into Vajrasattva’s nature in the previous text.
Guru Yoga of the White A: The universal visualization of purifying all beings through the practitioner’s yidam form parallels the White A’s light rays purifying sentient beings. Both practices simplify tantric complexity, focusing on universal compassion and non-dual dissolution (Bzhin gnas gnas pa’i dmigs pa).

Key Terms:

snying tig gi bla ma'i rnal 'byor: Guru Yoga of the Heart Essence, emphasizing the Khandro Nyingthig’s focus on universal compassion and non-dual awareness.
rdo rje 'chang chen po: Great Vajradhara, symbolizing the ultimate guru.
kun bzang yab yum: Samantabhadra and consort, the dharmakāya Adi Buddha.
o rgyan pad+ma: Orgyen Padma (Padmasambhava), central to Nyingma guru yoga.
badz+ra sa mA dza: Mantra invoking the assembly of deities.

Supplicatory Prayers:

The prayers invoke lineage figures and request blessings for realizing Dzogchen’s aims (rigpa, chos nyid, light body). They align with Brgyud pa’i zhing khams dmigs pa and Sems can gyi gnas yod dmigs pa, emphasizing lineage connection and universal liberation.

Conclusion

This Khandro Nyingthig Guru Yoga text emphasizes visualizing the practitioner as a yidam at the crown of all sentient beings, a distinctive feature of universal compassion (Sems can gyi gnas yod dmigs pa). The practice integrates a detailed lineage field (Brgyud pa’i zhing khams dmigs pa), guru visualization (Bla ma’i spyi bo’i dmigs pa), and dissolution into non-dual awareness (Bzhin gnas gnas pa’i dmigs pa), paralleling the White A’s universal purification. The supplicatory prayers reinforce lineage and ultimate realization.
